What

Privacy attacks include re-identification (linking anonymized data to real people), inference (guessing sensitive traits), tracking (cookies, device fingerprints), and correlation across datasets. Attack modeling anticipates these and designs defenses.

Why It Matters

Public datasets, partner feeds, and logs can combine into a deanonymization cocktail. Proactive modeling stops accidental exposure and partner overreach.

Example

  • Retail datasets: Basket + timestamp + ZIP can re-ID households; add DP noise, generalize timestamps/locations, and drop rare SKUs.
  • Fingerprinting: Block ‚Äúsuper-cookies‚Äù; randomize IDs; restrict third-party scripts.

How (Starter Pack)

  • Run LINDDUN and augment with re-ID threat tests (k-anonymity, l-diversity checks).
  • Apply differential privacy for published stats; limit query cadence.
  • Adopt Content Security Policy and script allow-lists; monitor third-party calls.
  • Use tokenization and rotate identifiers across contexts.
