What

A great privacy policy explains what you collect, why you collect it, who you share it with, how long you keep it, and how users can exercise rights. GDPR requires concise, intelligible, and easily accessible information.

Why It Matters

Transparent policies reduce complaints, improve consent quality, and demonstrate accountability‚Äîone of GDPR‚Äôs core principles.

Example

  • Large retailers: Long-form legal text + layered summaries and FAQs.
  • Indie apps: Short, human-language policy with clear contact and DSR instructions.

How (Starter Pack)

  • Use layered notices (summary on top, detail below).
  • Include DSR links (access/delete/opt-out) and retention timelines.
  • List processors/partners categories and cross-border transfers.
  • Version your policy; keep a change log.
