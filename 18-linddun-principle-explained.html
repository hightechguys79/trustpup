Seven lenses to reveal privacy risks hiding in plain sight.

What

LINDDUN is a privacy threat modeling framework: LinkabilityIdentifiabilityNon-repudiationDetectabilityDisclosure of informationUnawarenessNon-compliance. Use it with a data flow diagram to ask, ‚ÄúHow could this system leak or misuse personal data?‚Äù

Why It Matters

It gives teams a shared checklist to find non-obvious privacy failures (e.g., correlating pseudonymous events to re-identify people) and map them to actionable mitigations.

Example (Retail Loyalty)

  • Linkability: Same token across apps? Use rotating IDs per context.
  • Identifiability: Purchase logs + rare items may identify a household; aggregate and bucket rare categories.
  • Non-repudiation: Excessive logging can tie actions too tightly to a person; consider ephemeral IDs for low-risk flows.
  • Detectability: Network patterns may reveal presence; add padding/noise to sensitive endpoints.
  • Disclosure: Limit fields in partner feeds; encrypt at rest and in transit.
  • Unawareness: Add layered notices and just-in-time prompts.
  • Non-compliance: Map flows to GDPR/CCPA/Law 25; fix gaps before launch.

How (Starter Pack)

  • Draw a DFD; enumerate stores, flows, actors.
  • Run LINDDUN questions per flow; record risks & severities.
  • Apply mitigations: minimization, pseudonymization, access limits, DP, consent, policy.
  • Re-run after major changes; link outcomes to PIAs and audit logs.
