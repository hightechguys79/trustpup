Secure Knowledge Systems

RAG systems with cryptographic memory protection

What

Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) with selective disclosure. Documents are encrypted at rest, decrypted per request, and filtered by attribute-based access control (ABAC) and consent. The system cites sources and prevents disallowed fields from being surfaced.

Why It Matters

Naïve RAG can over-return sensitive passages. Combining cryptography, ABAC, and redaction ensures only minimal, permitted text is returned—and access can be revoked instantly by disabling decryption keys.

Example

  • Policy Q&A: Employees ask policy questions; RAG returns least-privilege snippets with source links. If consent is withdrawn or a doc is revoked, future requests cannot decrypt it.

How (Starter Pack)

  • Encrypt documents with KMS/HSM; rotate keys; keep per-document or per-tenant keys.
  • Gate retrieval and final rendering by ABAC (department, region, role, purpose).
  • Pseudonymise identifiers in embeddings; never embed raw SSNs/emails.
  • Maintain live revocation (key disablement) and signed, time-stamped citations.
