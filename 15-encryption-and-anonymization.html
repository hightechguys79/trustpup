Encrypt for confidentiality; anonymize for safe analytics; tokenize for operations.

What

Encryption protects data in transit (TLS 1.2+) and at rest (AES-256). Anonymization removes or generalizes identifiers so individuals can‚Äôt be singled out; pseudonymization/tokenization swaps identifiers with surrogates.

Why It Matters

Crypto reduces breach impact; anonymization enables sharing/analytics with lower risk. Beware re-identification: combine with aggregation, k-anonymity/l-diversity, or differential privacy for robust protection.

Example

  • Amazon/Shopify: Payment data encrypted at rest + in transit; keys in KMS/HSM.
  • Retail analytics: Replace emails with tokens; share only aggregated, anonymized results with partners.

How (Starter Pack)

  • Enforce TLS everywhere and AES-256 at rest; rotate keys; split duties.
  • Use format-preserving tokenization for operational systems.
  • Anonymize exports: generalize dates/locations, drop rare categories, and add noise.
  • Threat-model re-ID risks; test with synthetic linkage attempts.
