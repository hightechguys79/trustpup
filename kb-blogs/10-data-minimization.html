Data Minimization Collect Less, Risk Less

Only collect what you need—future you (and your customers) will thank you.

What

Data minimization means collecting and processing only the personal data that’s necessary for a specific, declared purpose. No extras “just in case.”

Why It Matters

Less data = smaller breach blast radius, faster DSR fulfillment, and lower storage/processing costs. It also aligns with GDPR’s core principles.

Example

  • Retail delivery: Ask for location only to schedule a delivery window—don’t track background GPS by default.
  • Sign-up forms: Make birthday optional unless it’s essential (e.g., age-restricted goods).

How (Starter Pack)

  • Map each field to a purpose; remove fields that lack a purpose.
  • Set default retention limits and auto-delete policies.
  • Replace raw data with tokens or aggregates where possible.
  • Run quarterly “data diet” reviews to prune bloat.
