GDPR Overview EU Standard

The global yardstick: principles, rights, accountability—and big penalties if you ghost the rules.

What

GDPR requires a lawful basis for each processing purpose (consent, contract, legal obligation, vital interests, public task, legitimate interests), plus transparency, data minimization, purpose & storage limitation, integrity/confidentiality, and accountability. Users have strong rights: access, rectification, erasure, portability, restriction, and objection.

Why It Matters

Applies extraterritorially to organizations processing EU residents’ data; fines can reach €20M or 4% of global revenue. It also sets best-practice expectations globally.

Example

  • Amazon EU: Get opt-in for certain cookies/marketing; provide download & erasure tools.
  • Spotify EU: GDPR-compliant data export for users (listening history, playlists).

How (Starter Pack)

  • Map each data flow to a lawful basis and document it.
  • Implement a DSR portal; track deadlines (often 30 days).
  • Enforce minimization & retention limits; write them into policy + code.
  • Appoint a DPO where required; keep Records of Processing Activities (RoPA).
