Consent Management Informed • Specific • Revocable

“I agree” should mean something—clear choices, real control, and proof you respected it.

What

Consent management is the process of collecting, storing, and honoring a person’s choices about how their data is used. Under GDPR, valid consent is freely given, specific, informed, and unambiguous, and it must be as easy to withdraw as to give.

Why It Matters

Granular, logged consent prevents “surprise” uses and reduces legal risk. It also improves UX trust—people are far more likely to opt in when the value exchange is obvious and the exit is painless.

Example

  • Retail apps: Separate toggles for email offers, personalized ads, and location for delivery. Each clearly explains why it’s needed.
  • Web sites: Cookie banners with Reject All and Manage Preferences, not just one giant “Accept.”

How (Starter Pack)

  • Adopt a Consent Management Platform (CMP); log purpose + timestamp + identity (or pseudonym).
  • Make withdrawal one click (Settings → Privacy → Withdraw) and propagate to downstream systems.
  • Use layered language: short summary + “learn more.” Avoid bundled consent.
  • Respect browser signals (where applicable) and document your policy.

References
