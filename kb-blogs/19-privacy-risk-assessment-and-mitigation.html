Privacy Risk Assessment & Mitigation Impact × Likelihood

Score the risks, pick the controls, prove they worked. Rinse and repeat.

What

A privacy risk assessment evaluates how likely personal data could be exposed/misused and the impact if it happens. Use a matrix to score likelihood × impact, then choose technical and organizational controls to reduce both. Often paired with a PIA/DPIA for higher-risk processing.

Why It Matters

Resources are finite; risk scoring helps you prioritize what to fix first. It also creates an audit trail showing you identified risks and implemented controls—gold for regulators and customers.

Example

  • Amazon-like purchase data: High sensitivity (behavioral + payment linkage). Mitigate with minimization, tokenization, access limits, and DP for analytics.
  • Walmart loyalty + location: Linkability risk—rotate IDs, coarsen geolocation, shorten retention.

How (Starter Pack)

  • List assets (PII, sensitive, behavioral), threats, vulnerabilities, likelihood/impact ratings.
  • Choose mitigations: encryption, pseudonymization, DP, RBAC/ABAC, vendor DPAs, purpose limits, retention cuts.
  • Track residual risk and owners; re-score after each control.
  • Tie results to PIAs/DPIAs and quarterly reviews.
