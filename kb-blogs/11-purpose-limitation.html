Purpose Limitation No Surprise Uses

Tell people why you collect data—and stick to it.

What

Purpose limitation means you only use personal data for the specific purposes you declared at collection. New use? Get a new lawful basis (often fresh consent) or don’t do it.

Why It Matters

Prevents “scope creep” that erodes trust and invites regulatory scrutiny. It’s a cornerstone of GDPR’s fairness and transparency principles.

Example

  • Retail browsing history: OK for on-site recommendations; not OK to share with unrelated ad brokers without consent.
  • Delivery address: Use for shipping and returns—not for unrelated marketing unless the user opts in.

How (Starter Pack)

  • Tag datasets with a declared purpose in your data catalog.
  • Block queries that mix incompatible purposes.
  • Run change reviews to catch “purpose drift.”
  • Capture new consent if you need to expand a purpose.
