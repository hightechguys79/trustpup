Privacy Vaults & Personal Data Stores Lock the Keys

Centralize sensitive bits in a hardened vault; use tokens elsewhere. Users stay in control.

What

A privacy vault (or personal data store) is a hardened service for storing identifiers and sensitive fields with fine-grained access, audit trails, and consent gates. Downstream apps use tokens or pseudonyms, not the raw PII.

Why It Matters

Concentrating the crown jewels allows strict controls (HSMs, KMS, policy enforcement) and simplifies “right to be forgotten” by deleting or detaching tokens. Vaults limit internal sprawl of PII.

Example

  • Loyalty programs: Store name, email, phone in the vault; give marketing a token that resolves only when consent exists.
  • Retail data science: Analysts work on tokenized events; vault resolves identities only for approved fulfillment operations.

How (Starter Pack)

  • Pick/build a vault with HSM-backed keys, RBAC/ABAC, and immutable audit logs.
  • Issue opaque tokens for PII; rotate and revoke on demand.
  • Enforce consent checks on resolve/de-tokenization calls.
  • Automate DSR erasure: delete PII, invalidate tokens system-wide.

References
