What

A privacy strategy aligns laws, tech, and operations. It defines how your retail org collects, stores, processes, and shares data‚Äîsafely and lawfully‚Äîwhile enabling analytics and personalization.

Why It Matters

Retail spans e-commerce, loyalty, payments, and vendors galore. Without a strategy, you‚Äôll juggle incidents and audits forever. With one, privacy becomes a feature: faster approvals, fewer breaches, happier customers.

Example

  • DSR Portal: Self-serve access/erasure with identity verification and SLA tracking.
  • Analytics: Tokenized events + DP aggregates; federated personalization at the edge.
  • Vendors: DPAs, SCCs, and quarterly attestations; least-privilege access.

How (Starter Pack)

  • 1) Map the law: Build a regulatory matrix (GDPR, CCPA/CPRA, Law 25, LGPD, DPDP). Feature-flag consent/retention by region.
  • 2) Govern the data: Inventory PII; classify personal vs sensitive; set retention & purpose tags.
  • 3) Engineer controls: Vault + tokenization, encryption, minimization, DP, FL; privacy-by-design in PRDs.
  • 4) Model threats: LINDDUN + risk matrix; fix high-risk flows before launch.
  • 5) Operationalize rights: DSR portal, consent registry, backup erasure.
  • 6) Measure & improve: KPIs (DSR SLA, consent coverage, deletion success); audits (ISO 27701); vendor reviews.
