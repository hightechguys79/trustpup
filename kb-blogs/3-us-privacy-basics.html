US Privacy Basics CCPA/CPRA • HIPAA • COPPA

The U.S. is a patchwork quilt: different laws for different contexts—and California sets the pace.

What

  • CCPA/CPRA (California): Rights to know, delete, correct, and opt-out of sale/share. Notice at collection + “Do Not Sell/Share” link for covered businesses.
  • HIPAA: Health data privacy for covered entities (providers, plans) and business associates.
  • COPPA: Parental consent before collecting personal info from kids under 13.

Why It Matters

CA is huge—many brands adopt “California as baseline” nationwide. Getting it wrong invites AG actions, FTC scrutiny, and brand damage.

Example

  • Walmart/Amazon (CCPA): Californians can request deletion of purchase data and opt-out of sale/share of personal information.
  • Pharmacy counters (HIPAA): Strict controls over prescription data and disclosures.
  • Kids’ ecommerce (COPPA): Obtain verifiable parental consent before tracking or profiling children.

How (Starter Pack)

  • Publish a “Do Not Sell/Share My Personal Information” page; honor opt-out signals.
  • Stand up a DSR workflow (access, deletion, correction) with identity verification.
  • Train staff on HIPAA & COPPA triggers; segment systems with PHI and kids’ data.
  • Maintain vendor DPAs and ensure downstream compliance.
