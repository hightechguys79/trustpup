What

A data breach is unauthorized access/exfiltration of personal data. Root causes range from credential stuffing to third-party compromise to memory-scraping malware on POS devices.

Why It Matters

Breaches bring costs (forensics, notifications, fines), operational disruption, and trust loss. Strong preparation (segmentation, least privilege, detection) turns disasters into containable incidents.

Example

  • Target (2013): POS malware exposed ~40M cards ‚Üí lessons: network segmentation, vendor access controls, rapid detection/response.
  • Yahoo (2013‚Äì2014 disclosed 2016): Massive account compromise ‚Üí lessons: credential hygiene, encryption of sensitive fields, continuous monitoring.

How (Starter Pack)

  • Encrypt at rest/in transit; tokenize payment data; segment networks.
  • Adopt least privilege; rotate keys/credentials; enforce MFA.
  • Monitor with anomaly detection; run tabletop exercises; prepare breach comms templates.
  • Maintain a vendor risk program and incident notification clauses.
