Privacy Monitoring & Auditing Trust, But Verify

Dashboards, logs, and audits turn privacy promises into proof.

What

Monitoring tracks how personal data is accessed and used (alerts for unusual queries, excessive exports). Auditing periodically reviews controls, policies, and evidence (DSR logs, consent records, retention deletes) to ensure compliance.

Why It Matters

Continuous oversight catches drift—shadow pipelines, purpose creep, expired consents. Audits create artifacts you’ll need for regulators, customers, and certifications.

Example

  • Walmart-style program: Quarterly audits of access logs, DSR SLA metrics, and vendor attestations.
  • Data lake: Alerts when PII tables are queried by unfamiliar service accounts or at odd hours.

How (Starter Pack)

  • Centralize access logs; enable anomaly detection and alert routing.
  • Schedule audits: consent coverage, DSR cycle times, retention deletes, vendor DPAs.
  • Adopt ISO/IEC 27701 (privacy ISMS) layered on ISO 27001.
  • Produce quarterly compliance reports for leadership.

References

  • ISO/IEC 27701 (Privacy Information Management): iso.org
  • NIST Privacy Framework (Monitor-P, Govern-P): nist.gov/privacy-framework
