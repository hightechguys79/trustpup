Privacy KPIs Measure to Improve

What gets measured gets managed—and defended during audits.

What

Privacy KPIs are quantifiable signals that your program is working: DSR cycle times, consent coverage, deletion success rate, minimization wins, incident MTTR, vendor attestation status.

Why It Matters

KPIs reveal bottlenecks (e.g., slow erasure), drive investment (more automation), and prove accountability to leadership and regulators.

Example Dashboard Tiles

  • DSR SLA: % requests closed within 30 days.
  • Consent Coverage: % active users with granular consents recorded.
  • Deletion Success: % erase jobs completed across live + backups.
  • Minimization: # of fields removed / quarter; avg retention days.
  • Vendors: % with current DPAs/SCCs and security attestations.

How (Starter Pack)

  • Define 5–8 KPIs aligned to NIST PF and your risk register.
  • Automate data collection from DSR tools, consent logs, and data catalogs.
  • Publish a monthly privacy scorecard; assign owners for red metrics.
  • Review with execs; tie improvements to roadmap and budget.
