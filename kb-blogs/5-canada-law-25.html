Canada (Québec) Law 25 / Bill 64 Modernization

Québec’s privacy revamp elevates consent, PIAs, transparency, and penalties—bringing Canada closer to GDPR expectations.

What

Law 25 strengthens: meaningful consent, privacy impact assessments for high-risk processing, transparency, data governance, and breach notification. Non-compliance can trigger significant administrative monetary penalties tied to global revenue.

Why It Matters

If you operate in Québec or process data about Québec residents, you must align collection notices, consent, and PIAs with Law 25’s staged requirements.

Example

  • Retailers (e.g., loyalty programs): Granular, plain-language opt-ins for personalized offers and data sharing with partners.
  • Banks & fintech: Conduct PIAs for new analytics models (e.g., AI scoring) before launch.

How (Starter Pack)

  • Establish a PIA workflow for new/changed systems.
  • Rewrite consent and privacy notices in clear French/English; include purpose, retention, and contact.
  • Set up breach notification playbooks and thresholds.
  • Track third-party disclosures; sign data processing/transfer agreements.
