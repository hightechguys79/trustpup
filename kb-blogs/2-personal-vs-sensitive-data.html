🐶 Personal Data vs Sensitive Data Foundations

Not all data is created equal: some bits say who you are; others reveal what could seriously harm you if exposed.

What

Personal data is anything that identifies you (name, email, phone, loyalty ID, device ID). Sensitive data (a.k.a. GDPR “special categories”) includes health details, biometrics, precise location, racial/ethnic origin, sexual orientation, political opinions, religious beliefs, and certain financial/medical records. It’s the difference between knowing “you’re Alex” and knowing “Alex’s heart condition + medications.”

Why It Matters

Sensitive data carries higher risk if misused—discrimination, targeted scams, blackmail, or financial/medical harms. Laws typically require stronger safeguards, explicit consent, and tighter access for sensitive categories.

Example

  • Fitness app: Heart rate & mental health notes = sensitive.
  • Retail loyalty: Email & shopping list = personal. Still protected, but risk profile differs.
  • Walmart pharmacy: Purchase history (personal) can cross into HIPAA territory when tied to prescriptions (sensitive).

How (Starter Pack)

  • Classify each field (personal vs sensitive) in your data inventory.
  • Encrypt sensitive categories at rest & in transit; use key rotation.
  • Limit access to least-privileged roles; enable just-in-time access for support.
  • Require explicit consent before collecting/processing sensitive data; log the proof.
  • Shorten retention and set auto-deletion timers for high-risk fields.
