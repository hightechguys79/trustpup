Consent Fatigue & Best Practices Fewer, Clearer Prompts

When everything asks for consent, nothing gets read. Earn attention with clarity and timing.

What

Consent fatigue happens when users see too many prompts or legalese. Valid consent must be informed, specific, and revocable—but it shouldn’t be exhausting.

Why It Matters

Overloading users leads to blind acceptance or blanket rejection—both hurt data quality and trust. Regulators also frown on manipulative UX (“dark patterns”).

Example

  • Retail app: Single, clean screen with 3 toggles (email offers, personalized ads, location-for-delivery) + “learn more.”
  • Web banners: Equal-weight Accept/Reject All + granular controls—no maze of modals.

How (Starter Pack)

  • Layered design: Short summary + link to details; avoid jargon.
  • Batch requests: Ask once per context; avoid repeated nudges.
  • Neutral choices: Same visual weight; no pre-ticked boxes.
  • Easy exits: One-tap withdraw; reflect across systems quickly.
