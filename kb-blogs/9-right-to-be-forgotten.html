🐶 Right to be Forgotten (Erasure) GDPR Art. 17

Sometimes the best feature is a clean slate.

What

The right to erasure lets people ask you to delete their personal data when it’s no longer needed, consent is withdrawn, or processing is unlawful—subject to lawful exceptions (e.g., tax or legal claims).

Why It Matters

Erasure reduces risk exposure and shows respect for user autonomy. It’s also time-bound—fulfill requests promptly and document exceptions.

Example

  • Account deletion: Retailers remove identifiers and purchase history; keep only anonymized aggregates for analytics.
  • Search results (case law context): Platforms may delist outdated or irrelevant results in specific jurisdictions.

How (Starter Pack)

  • Build an erasure orchestration pipeline: live systems → backups → vendors.
  • Log lawful exceptions; respond with rationale when you can’t fully delete.
  • Confirm completion and provide a summary of what was removed.
  • Test restores to ensure deleted data doesn’t reappear from backups.

References

  • EDPB/DPAs – guidance on erasure & exceptions: edpb.europa.eu
